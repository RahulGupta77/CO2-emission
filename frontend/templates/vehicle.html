Transport accounts for around one-fifth of global CO₂ emissions.Most of this comes from passenger vehicles – cars and buses – which contribute 45.1%. The other 29.4% comes from trucks carrying freight. Since the entire transport sector accounts for 21% of total emissions, and road transport accounts for three-quarters of transport emissions, road transport accounts for 15% of total CO2 emissions. Our analysis shows that the sales-weighted industry average CO2 emissions in FY 2018–19 from new passenger cars in India was 121.9 g/km

Here are some ways to decrease CO₂ emission:

  1. Getting a PUC(Pollution Under Control) certificate. This helps in determining that the emissions coming from your vehicle are within the permissible limits.
  2. Reducing the overall weight of your vehicle might help in less fuel consumption as more weight means more work for the engine.
  3. Using better Fuel helps in improving fuel efficiency and reducing emissions. This is due to the fact that These types of fuels contain active cleaning agents to remove dirt from the engine.
  4. Regular check up of Air filter might decrease multitude of issues,If an engine can't breathe, deposits will build up, causing premature wear and tear.
  5. Limiting the idle time will save you a lot of fuel as restarting takes less fuel than idling for 10 second.
